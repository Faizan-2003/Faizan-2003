Goal-Line Technology (GLT)

Goal-Line Technology debuted in the 2014 World Cup in Brazil and it was also an important part if the tech used a couple of years ago in Russia at the 2018 FIFA World Cup. With Goal-Line Technology(GLT), 14 cameras capture up to 500 frames per second and send the image to an image processing system. The 3D coordinates of the ball are monitored, and when the entire ball crosses the goal line the camera capture it and sends a signal to the referee's watch. Goal Control is still providing the technology throughout significant football competitions around the globe, as it did in Brazil.

Video Assistant Referee (VAR)

A new generation of video technology was used in the 2018 FIFA World Cup in Russia, Called Video Assistant Referee, to assist in officiating. The VAR team supports teh referees form a centralised video operations room at the international broadcast centre of the stadium. One video Assistant referee, three assistants, and four replay operators make the group, which has access to all 33 camera feeds covering the matches, plus exclusive access to two special offside cameras. Two cameras are turned on the refs themselves, so their decision process will be public. The tech is only used in situations where a call may have changed the result of the match. If a "Clear and obvious error" has been made, the team communicates with the on-field refs and they can stop at any time to consult with the VAR team.

Electronic Performance and Tracking Systems (EPTS)

Electronic Performance and Tracking Systems (EPTS), which include camera-based and wearbale technologies, are used to control and improve player and team Performance. EPTS primarily track player and ball positions but can also be used in combination with microelectronic devices (such as accelerometers and gyroscopes) and heart-rate monitors, as well as other devices to measure load or physiological parameters. These devies are used in combination with Micro Electrical Mechanical devices (such as accelerometers, gyroscopes and compasses) to provide inertial load and other medical information. Each team has three EPTS devices. One is for the analyst in the stands, another is for the analyst on the bench, and the third is for the medical team.
